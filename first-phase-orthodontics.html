First-Phase Orthodontics

Your child's wellbeing and happiness sit at the centre of everything we do. Often we are the first dental home a child ever has, and that gives us a chance to build trust from the very beginning.

Positive experiences in those early years encourage children to take responsibility for their dental health as they grow. It is a role we take seriously, in an office that runs green and completely paperless.

Why start early?

Between the ages of six and thirteen, children grow quickly, and their mouths are busy places. Baby teeth are on the move, adult teeth are arriving, and the jaw itself is still taking shape.

Spotting orthodontic needs during this window is known as interceptive, or first-phase, orthodontics. Because treatment works alongside your child's natural growth instead of against it, starting early can help shorten the length of any comprehensive orthodontic work needed later on.

What early treatment can help with

1

Thumb and finger habits. Gently guiding a child past a persistent sucking habit can help protect the position of the front teeth.

2

Bite and jaw relationships. Addressing how the upper and lower jaws meet can head off functional problems as well as appearance concerns.

3

Narrow arches. Widening a narrow dental arch can create the room adult teeth will need to come in well.

4

Prominent front teeth. Front teeth that sit well forward face a higher chance of injury, so bringing them into line early can be protective as well as cosmetic.

5

Crowded or overlapping teeth. Sorting out crowding while the mouth is still growing can make later alignment simpler.

A team approach: Dr. Anu works together with a network of orthodontists to tailor each child's treatment. Having several specialists to draw on also gives families more choice when it comes to scheduling.

Meet your dentist

Dr. Anu Korada

Dr. Anu trained in the UK and was drawn to pediatric dentistry early in her career by the fun, innocence and honesty of children. A mother of two herself, she moved to Canada from London with her family and brings more than ten years of experience in pediatric and special care dentistry across both countries. She teaches pediatric dentistry at UBC and BC Children's Hospital.

She believes good oral hygiene is the gateway to good overall general health, and that baby teeth matter: they support the growing jaw and face and hold the space adult teeth will need.
